Aristotle: a foundational software ecosystem

Aristotle is an open-source software ecosystem for predictive biology developed by the Weissman lab, encompassing a comprehensive collection of toolkits catering to various domains such as metabolic labeling data processing, RNA velocity field analysis, spatial transcriptomics, and quantitative modeling of single-cell genomics.

Current toolkits includes metabolic labeling data preprocessing pipeline Dynast (tutorial), dynamics analytical and predictive framework Dynamo (tutorial), quantitative spatiotemporal modeling framework Spateo (tutorial).

Genome-Wide Perturb-Seq

Perturb-seq is an experimental approach to map the transcriptional effects of genetic perturbations by combining CRISPR-based genetic screening with information-rich, single-cell RNA-sequencing phenotypes.

Applied at genome-scale, Perturb-seq enables systematic prediction of gene function and in-depth study of complex cellular phenotypes such as aneuploidy, differentiation, RNA processing, and stress-specific regulation of the mitochondrial genome.
